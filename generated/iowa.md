# Iowa

Iowa is a state located in the Midwestern region of the United States, situated between the Mississippi River on its eastern border and the Missouri River on its western boundary. Admitted to the Union on December 28, 1846, as the 29th state, Iowa covers approximately 56,273 square miles and is bordered by six states: Wisconsin and Illinois to the east, Missouri to the south, Nebraska and South Dakota to the west, and Minnesota to the north. The state capital and largest city is Des Moines, located in the south-central portion of the state. Iowa's landscape is characterized by gently rolling plains and fertile prairies, with the majority of its terrain shaped by glacial activity during the last ice age, resulting in some of the richest agricultural soil in the world.

Geographically, Iowa sits in the heart of the Great Plains and Midwest agricultural belt, making it one of the most productive farming regions in North America. The state's location placed it directly in the path of westward expansion during the 19th century, serving as a critical transition zone between the established eastern states and the frontier territories beyond the Missouri River. This central position made Iowa particularly vulnerable to natural disasters that swept across the Great Plains, including the devastating Grasshopper Plague of 1873-1877, which severely impacted the state's agricultural communities alongside neighboring states like Minnesota, Nebraska, Kansas, and Missouri.

Iowa's administrative structure divides the state into 99 counties, with major urban centers including Des Moines, Cedar Rapids, Davenport, Sioux City, and Iowa City. The state's economy has historically been dominated by agriculture, particularly corn and soybean production, though manufacturing, insurance, and financial services have become increasingly important sectors. Iowa's geographic position in the American heartland has given it significant political importance, particularly as the site of the first presidential caucuses in the United States electoral process.
