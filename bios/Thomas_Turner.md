# Thomas Turner

Key life events:

- 1876 — Born on the Turner homestead in Osceola Township, Iowa, the youngest child of Elias and Margaret Turner.
- 1883 — Survived a serious bout of scarlet fever, which left him with partial hearing loss in his left ear.
- 1888 — Became known locally for repairing tools and simple farm machinery, showing early mechanical aptitude.
- 1894 — Left home at age 18 to apprentice with a blacksmith in nearby Chariton, Iowa, learning forging, wheelwrighting, and farm-equipment repair.
- 1901 — Opened “Turner & Co. Smithy and Machine Works”, servicing farms throughout Lucas County.
- 1917 — Enlisted in the U.S. Army Ordnance Corps during World War I, stationed at Camp Dodge (Des Moines), where he maintained artillery and transport equipment.
- 1919 — Returned home and expanded his shop, adding early gasoline-engine repair as tractors spread across the Midwest.
- 1924 — Married Helen Sutter, a widowed schoolteacher with one young son, forming a blended family.
- 1936 — Served on the county relief board during the Great Depression, repairing equipment for struggling farmers at reduced or no cost.
- 1943 — Passed away in Chariton, Iowa, leaving the workshop to his stepson, who continued the Turner mechanical tradition.
