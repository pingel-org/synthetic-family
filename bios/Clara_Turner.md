# Clara Turner

- 1871 — Born in Osceola Township, Iowa, the first child of Elias and Margaret Turner, delivered in the family’s original one-room cabin.
- 1878 — Began attending the township’s first schoolhouse, walking nearly two miles each way with neighboring children.
- 1886 — Took on household responsibilities after a harsh winter, helping care for her younger siblings and livestock.
- 1890 — Earned her teaching certificate from the Normal School in Oskaloosa, Iowa, becoming one of the township’s few formally trained educators.
- 1891 — Began teaching at the Osceola School, known for her strict but fair classroom and her insistence on nature study and handwriting practice.
- 1897 — Married farmhand Jonathan Keating after meeting him at a church harvest supper; the couple later established a small 40-acre farm nearby.
- 1903 — Elected secretary of the Osceola Women’s Temperance and Literacy Society, following her mother’s involvement in its founding.
- 1915 — Wrote a series of essays on rural childhood and pioneer life, later published in the Iowa Farmer’s Advocate.
- 1934 — Retired from teaching, but continued tutoring children during the Dust Bowl years.
- 1954 — Passed away at her daughter’s home in Chariton, Iowa, leaving behind diaries that became a valuable family record.
